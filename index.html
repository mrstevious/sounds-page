By Stephen Crawford

\

How Does Sound Travel?

\

Sound moves through the air as a series of vibrations of the air molecules. These tiny motions pass longitudinally from one molecule to the next, which in turn oscillate the parts of the ear that sense sound. These vibrations can be represented as a wave in which the amplitude represents the volume of the sound and the frequency the pitch. These are analogue waves which cannot be readily represented in digital systems such as computers

\

Sampling Sound

\

In order to represent the waves digitally, the computer must sample sections of the wave - at regular time intervals the frequency and amplitude of the wave are measured. This allows the computer to represent portions of the wave as discrete numbers and process them accordingly. The sampling rate will determine the quality of the digital recording - higher sampling rates result in better quality but larger files.\
